Planning a Session

A successful planning of an interpreted session should cover:

  • Session Context: Reason for the visit and medical history.
  • Logistics: Modality (in-person, video, phone) and room setup.
  • Roles & Expectations: Clarifying how the encounter will be patterned.

Pre-briefing

A successful pre-briefing with interpreters should cover:

  • Goal: The primary purpose of the interaction.
  • Jargon info: Expected medical terms to clarify.
  • Patient risk: Mental state and health literacy.
  • Culture question: Inviting interpreter insights on culture.
  • Turn-taking rule: Short, one-sentence pacing.

Pre-briefing with the patient

Good pre-briefing with the patient should FIRST confirm the interpretation method and devices usage:

  • Interpretation: Introduce names of the nurse and the interpreter.
  • Turn-taking rules: Keep sentences short and pause for interpreting.
  • Devices usage: Explain how to use the devices to listen and speak.
